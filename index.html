Kopi Luwak (Indonésia)
Price: Can range from $100 to $600 per pound (0.45 kg). Origin: Indonesia. Uniqueness: Kopi Luwak coffee is produced from beans that have been eaten and excreted by civets (a mammal similar to cats). The animal’s digestive process alters the chemical composition of the beans, resulting in a smooth, less bitter taste.

Black Ivory (Tailândia)
Price: Around $500 to $1,500 per pound. Origin: Thailand. Uniqueness: Similar to Kopi Luwak, Black Ivory is produced from beans that pass through the digestive system of elephants. After digestion, the beans are collected from the feces, and it is believed that the natural digestive process creates an extremely smooth coffee with notes of chocolate and spices.

Hacienda La Esmeralda (Panamá)
Price: Can reach up to $350 per pound. Origin: Panama. Uniqueness: Famous for being cultivated on the slopes of Mount Baru, this Geisha variety coffee is highly valued for its complex aromatic profile, which includes floral and fruity notes. It frequently wins quality coffee competitions worldwide.

Ospina Dynasty Coffee (Colômbia)
Price: Between $150 and $700 per pound. Origin: Colombia. Uniqueness: Produced by one of the oldest coffee dynasties in the world, founded in the 19th century, this coffee is grown at high altitudes. It stands out for its smoothness, complexity of flavors, and velvety body, and it is hand-picked.
